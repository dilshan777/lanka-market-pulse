# IMF Review Scheduled for Next Week: Key Expectations for Sri Lanka

The **International Monetary Fund (IMF)** will conduct its **third review** of Sri Lanka's **Extended Fund Facility (EFF)** next week, a critical milestone that could unlock the next tranche of approximately **USD 337 million** in financial support.

## Review Schedule

- **Mission Arrival**: June 10, 2026
- **Technical Discussions**: June 10-14, 2026
- **Staff-Level Agreement**: Expected by June 20, 2026
- **Board Approval**: Targeted for July 2026

## Key Areas of Focus

### 1. Fiscal Consolidation

The IMF will assess Sri Lanka's progress toward its **primary balance surplus target** of 2.3% of GDP by 2026:

| Fiscal Indicator | Target | Actual (Q1 2026) | Status |
|------------------|--------|------------------|--------|
| Revenue/GDP | 14.5% | 13.8% | At Risk |
| Expenditure/GDP | 18.5% | 18.2% | On Track |
| Primary Balance | +2.3% | +1.8% | Needs Improvement |

### 2. Debt Restructuring Progress

**Bilateral creditors:**
- China: Framework agreement reached, details being finalized
- India: Debt restructuring terms agreed, documentation in progress
- Paris Club: Terms of reference signed

**Private creditors:**
- Bondholder committee formed
- Macro-linked bonds proposal under discussion
- Target: Agreement by Q3 2026

### 3. Structural Reforms

**Completed reforms:**
- ✅ Electricity tariff restructuring
- ✅ SOE governance framework
- ✅ Anti-corruption legislation enacted
- ✅ Public financial management reforms

**Ongoing reforms:**
- 🔄 SOE restructuring (SriLankan Airlines, CPC)
- 🔄 Revenue administration modernization
- 🔄 Public service reform
- 🔄 Land registration digitization

### 4. Reserve Accumulation

The CBSL has committed to building foreign reserves to **USD 6.5 billion** by end-2026:

- Current reserves: USD 5.8 billion
- Monthly accumulation target: USD 150-200 million
- Remittances and tourism supporting inflows

## Market Implications

**Positive outcomes expected:**
- Successful review → USD 337 million disbursement
- Improved investor confidence
- Potential credit rating upgrade
- Lower sovereign borrowing costs

**Risks to monitor:**
- Revenue shortfalls
- Delayed debt restructuring
- Political uncertainty
- External shocks

## Government Position

**State Minister of Finance, Mr. Shehan Semasinghe:**
> "We are confident of meeting all IMF program targets. The government remains committed to the reform agenda and fiscal consolidation path agreed with the Fund."

## Opposition Views

**SJB Economic Spokesperson, Dr. Harsha de Silva:**
> "While we support the IMF program, the government must ensure that reforms are implemented equitably. The burden of adjustment should not fall disproportionately on the poor."

## External Sector Performance

**Trade balance (Jan-May 2026):**
- Exports: USD 5.2 billion (+8.5% YoY)
- Imports: USD 8.1 billion (+3.2% YoY)
- Trade deficit: USD 2.9 billion (-9.4% YoY)

**Tourism:**
- Arrivals (Jan-May): 1.05 million (+22% YoY)
- Revenue: USD 1.4 billion (+18% YoY)

## Conclusion

The upcoming IMF review is a pivotal moment for Sri Lanka's economic recovery. Successful completion would reinforce the country's reform credentials and pave the way for sustained growth. Market participants will closely watch for any signals on the timing of debt restructuring completion and the potential for program extension.
