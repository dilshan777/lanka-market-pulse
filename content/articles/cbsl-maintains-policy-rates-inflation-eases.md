# CBSL Maintains Policy Rates at 8.25% as Inflation Eases to 3.2%

The **Central Bank of Sri Lanka (CBSL)** announced today that it will maintain its key policy interest rates unchanged at **8.25%**, citing easing inflationary pressures and stable domestic economic conditions.

## Inflation Continues to Ease

Headline inflation eased to **3.2% year-on-year in May 2026**, down from 3.6% in April, marking the lowest reading in over two years. The decline was primarily driven by:

- Lower food prices due to improved harvests
- Stable global commodity prices
- Favorable base effects
- Moderate demand conditions

**Core inflation** (excluding volatile food and energy items) stood at 2.8%, well within the CBSL's target range of 4-6%.

## Policy Decision Rationale

The Monetary Board, in its statement, noted:

> "The current monetary policy stance remains appropriate to support the ongoing economic recovery while maintaining price stability. The easing of inflationary pressures provides room for continued accommodative policy."

## Key Policy Parameters

| Indicator | Current | Previous | Change |
|-----------|---------|----------|--------|
| Standing Deposit Facility Rate | 7.50% | 7.50% | Unchanged |
| Standing Lending Facility Rate | 8.25% | 8.25% | Unchanged |
| Statutory Reserve Ratio | 2.00% | 2.00% | Unchanged |

## Economic Outlook

The CBSL maintained its GDP growth forecast for 2026 at **3.5-4.0%**, supported by:

1. **Tourism recovery**: Tourist arrivals expected to reach 2.5 million in 2026
2. **Remittances**: Steady growth in worker remittances
3. **Export performance**: Improved demand for key exports
4. **Infrastructure investment**: Ongoing development projects

## Market Reaction

The policy announcement was largely in line with market expectations. The Sri Lankan Rupee remained stable against the US Dollar, trading around **LKR 298.50**.

**Government securities market:**
- 91-day T-bill yield: 8.15% (-2 bps)
- 182-day T-bill yield: 8.45% (-3 bps)
- 364-day T-bill yield: 8.75% (-5 bps)

## IMF Program Update

The CBSL also confirmed that the **third review** of Sri Lanka's Extended Fund Facility (EFF) arrangement with the IMF is scheduled for next week. The review is expected to focus on:

- Fiscal consolidation progress
- Debt restructuring milestones
- Structural reform implementation
- Reserve accumulation targets

## Analyst Views

**Dr. Nimal Perera, Chief Economist at Lanka Research:**
> "The CBSL's decision to hold rates was widely expected. With inflation well-contained and the economy showing signs of stabilization, there's no immediate need for rate adjustments. The focus should remain on maintaining financial stability and supporting credit growth."

**Ms. Priyanka Fernando, Senior Analyst at Capital Markets:**
> "The stable policy environment is positive for equities. Banking stocks, in particular, should benefit from the sustained low-rate environment, which supports lending growth and asset quality."

## Conclusion

The CBSL's decision to maintain rates reflects confidence in the current economic trajectory. With inflation easing and growth momentum building, the monetary policy stance appears well-calibrated. Market participants will now turn their attention to the upcoming IMF review and quarterly corporate earnings.
