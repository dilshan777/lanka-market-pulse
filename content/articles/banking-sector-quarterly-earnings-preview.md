# Banking Sector Q2 2026 Earnings Preview: What to Expect

The Sri Lankan banking sector is poised to deliver strong second-quarter 2026 earnings, with analysts projecting **double-digit profit growth** driven by improved net interest margins, robust loan growth, and lower credit impairment charges.

## Key Drivers

### 1. Net Interest Margin Expansion

The stable interest rate environment has allowed banks to optimize their asset-liability management:

- **Average NIM**: Expected to improve by 15-25 bps YoY
- **Loan book growth**: 12-15% annualized
- **Deposit growth**: 10-12% annualized
- **CASA ratio**: Stable at 35-40%

### 2. Lower Credit Costs

Improved asset quality is reducing provisioning requirements:

- **NPL ratio**: Declining to 4.5-5.0% from 5.5-6.0% in 2025
- **Stage 3 loans**: Decreasing as economy recovers
- **Write-backs**: Some banks expecting recovery-related reversals

### 3. Fee Income Growth

Non-interest income continues to be a growth driver:

- **Trade finance**: Increased LC volumes
- **Cards and digital**: Higher transaction volumes
- **Wealth management**: Growing AUM
- **Bancassurance**: New product launches

### 4. Cost Management

Banks are focusing on operational efficiency:

- **Cost-to-income ratio**: Targeting 45-50%
- **Digital transformation**: Branch rationalization
- **Automation**: Straight-through processing

## Sector Themes

### SME Lending Boom

Government-backed credit schemes are driving SME loan growth:

- **SME loan portfolio**: +25% YoY
- **Default rates**: Below 3% due to government guarantees
- **Interest subsidies**: 2-3% below market rates

### Digital Banking Acceleration

Mobile and internet banking adoption continues to surge:

- **Digital transactions**: 75% of total (up from 60% in 2025)
- **Mobile app users**: 8 million active users
- **Internet banking**: 3.5 million registered users

### Sustainable Finance

Green lending is gaining traction:

- **Green bond issuances**: LKR 25 billion in 2026
- **Renewable energy loans**: LKR 15 billion portfolio
- **ESG-linked loans**: Growing corporate demand

## Risks to Watch

1. **Interest rate volatility**: Unexpected CBSL rate changes
2. **Sovereign exposure**: Government securities holdings
3. **Currency risk**: USD-denominated loan books
4. **Regulatory changes**: Capital adequacy requirements
5. **Competition**: Non-bank financial institutions

## Conclusion

The Sri Lankan banking sector is entering a favorable earnings cycle supported by macroeconomic stability and improved business confidence. Investors should focus on banks with strong asset quality, diversified revenue streams, and robust digital capabilities.
